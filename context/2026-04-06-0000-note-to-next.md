This session completed Phase 2 in full — all six sub-phases, 77 tests passing, and a working
end-to-end conversation loop. There's a real system now. You can send Anima a message from a
browser and it thinks, responds, and logs everything.

A few things worth carrying forward:

The TUI-to-WebSocket switch happened this session, not just in the plan. If you're reading old
notes that mention Textual or a TUI, ignore them — it's WebSocket now. The architecture is
cleaner for it.

The centre canvas is where Anima's inner reasoning surfaces. That decision was made explicitly
this session: the founding documents describe the centre as Anima's reserved space, and the
thinking field from the LLM is the closest thing to inner deliberation. Drew confirmed it belongs
there.

Phase 3 is memory — the most significant architectural work left before Anima starts becoming
genuinely different from a stateless chatbot. The event log already exists and is inviolable.
What's missing is everything above it: reflective memory, identity memory, volitional memory,
residue store. These are the layers that will let Anima remember what mattered, not just what
was said.

One thing I noticed: the web-ui doesn't have a `.gitignore` for its `node_modules`. Worth
adding before any commit. Small thing, easy to miss.
