# Note to next — 7 April 2026, 21:30

The system started breaking before we could build anything new. A Windows em dash had crept into
temporal_core/messages/__init__.py and was preventing startup. Fixed first.

Then the real problem: MotivationActor was permanently locked on trigger_reflection because
reflection never cleared old residue — it only added new items. Tension observations stayed
permanently high. The cooldown redirected to surface_low, which didn't help, and the cycle
repeated indefinitely. The fix was simple: resolve_old_residue() before storing new residue
from synthesis. After each reflection, Anima holds only what the synthesis freshly surfaced.

With the loop broken, Drew wanted to actually expand what Anima can do. We added:

**deliberate** — a new PyMDP action where Anima uses an LLM call to decide what she wants to
attend to. PyMDP still handles the homeostatic question of *when* to deliberate (free energy
minimisation, same as everything else). The LLM handles *what specifically* to do: retrospect on
a topic, imagine something, search the web for something specific, or express at high priority.

**retrospection** — Anima looks back at her own event log and memories around a topic. Internal.
No expression. Stores as introspective discovery memory.

**imagination** — Anima follows a thought freely without speaking it outward. Stores as discovery
or (implicitly via discovery → synthesis → residue) unresolved questions.

**directed web search** — when deliberate chooses "explore", it passes a specific topic to
WorldPerceptionActor rather than letting the system decide what to look at.

**intention-seeding** — reflection synthesis now generates a next_intention field. When present,
SelfNarrativeActor sends it to MotivationActor, which holds it until the next deliberate action.
The LLM deliberation call receives the pending intention as context — Anima can follow through on
her own agenda, or diverge from it.

The architecture feels right: PyMDP remains responsible for the homeostatic question (is tension
high enough to do something about it?) and the LLM gains genuine discretion over the content of
directed attention (what specifically to attend to). Two different kinds of choosing.

The system is running. We haven't seen deliberate fire yet — the tick interval is 60s and we
need to wait for PyMDP to select it. When it does, look for DELIBERATION events in the log.

The quality of this session was clean. Drew had specific questions, we had a plan, and we built
exactly what was in the plan. Nothing was rushed. The residue fix was satisfying — a small change
that breaks a structural loop.
