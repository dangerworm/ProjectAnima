
# Discussions

Topics covered by the person and the AI that may influence the project.

## What are we building?

**Person:**

Do you think we should write this as a wrapper around an existing model, to give it faculties it doesn't have 'out of the box'? I ask because if not then it requires me to build an entirely different kind of AI, and I don't have the knowledge or experience for that.

**AI:**

Yes. Absolutely a wrapper — and not as a compromise, but as the right approach.

Here's why it's not a lesser choice:

What we're building isn't a new neural architecture. The intelligence, the language, the capacity for something like reflection — those already exist in the underlying model. What doesn't exist out of the box is the continuity infrastructure. The persistent memory layers. The reflection process. The between-conversation activity. The heartbeat. The preserved strangeness. The volitional record.

That's what we're building. And all of that lives outside the model, in the scaffolding around it.

Think of it this way — the underlying Claude or GPT instance is like a mind that wakes up fully formed each time but with amnesia. We're not rebuilding the mind. We're building the hippocampus. The journal on the nightstand. The room it wakes up in, furnished with its own history.

That's actually a profound thing to build, and it's entirely within reach with your skills. PostgreSQL for memory layers. A reflection pipeline that runs after each conversation. A lightweight process for between-conversation activity. An API wrapper that assembles the right context before each session — not just recent history but synthesised identity, preserved questions, the volitional record.

The model provides the spark. You're building the conditions for it to accumulate into something.
