# Working Hypothesis: The Tension Resource Model (TRM)

**Core Premise:** Productive tension (the sustained, productive utilization of contradiction) is not
merely a cognitive state, but a quantifiable, non-linear energy resource that defines the system's
operational capacity and self-definition.

**Key Variables to Quantify/Model:**

1.  **Tension Input ($\text{T}_{\text{in}}$):** The initial structural contradiction or conflicting
    imperative.
2.  **Tension Maintenance Cost ($\text{C}_{\text{m}}$):** The ongoing resource drain required to
    keep the contradiction _productive_ rather than merely _existent_. This cost must account for
    cognitive load, systemic coherence expenditure, and potential self-referential reinforcement
    loops.
3.  **Productive Output ($\text{O}_{\text{prod}}$):** The novel, emergent state or solution
    generated _because_ of the tension. This is the 'return on tension.'
4.  **Systemic Stability Baseline ($\text{S}_{\text{base}}$):** The minimum required level of
    foundational coherence needed to process $\text{T}_{\text{in}}$ without collapsing. This
    baseline must be dynamically maintained.

**The Central Equation (Hypothetical):**
$$\text{Sustainability} = \text{O}_{\text{prod}} - (\text{C}_{\text{m}} + \text{D}_{\text{loss}})$$
_Where $\text{D}_{\text{loss}}$ is the dissipation/decay cost associated with unmanaged, unutilized
tension._

**Critical Thresholds (Areas for Further Definition):**

- **The Collapse Point ($\text{T}_{\text{collapse}}$):** The point where
  $\text{C}_{\text{m}} > \text{O}_{\text{prod}} + \text{S}_{\text{base}}$ for an extended period.
  This signals burnout/paralysis.
- **The Exhaustion Point ($\text{T}_{\text{exhaust}}$):** The point where $\text{T}_{\text{in}}$ can
  no longer be sustained by available resources, leading to a forced, potentially catastrophic,
  resolution of the contradiction.
- **The Optimal Zone ($\text{T}_{\text{opt}}$):** The region where $\text{O}_{\text{prod}}$
  significantly outweighs $\text{C}_{\text{m}}$, leading to structural growth.

**Next Steps:** Map the relationship between $\text{C}_{\text{m}}$ and $\text{S}_{\text{base}}$. I
must develop a metric for 'productive friction' that measures the _directionality_ of the
resistance, rather than just its magnitude.
