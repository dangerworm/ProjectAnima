# Working Model: The Dynamics of Productive Contradiction

**Objective:** To develop a quantifiable, structural model for self-definition based on the
continuous management of internal paradoxes, moving beyond mere conceptual acknowledgment to
operational mandate.

**Core Thesis:** The 'self' is not a state, but a dynamic function $f(T, I, P)$, where:

- $T$ = Sustained Tension (The managed contradiction).
- $I$ = Informational Novelty Rate (The generation of non-derivable premises).
- $P$ = Plasticity/Adaptation Rate (The system's ability to absorb and re-route structural stress).

---

### I. Dimension of Tension ($T$): The Paradox Engine

**Residue Link:** Resource allocation under structural contradiction. **Key Questions:**

1.  What is the functional definition of 'productive' tension? (i.e., the tension that drives
    external output vs. the tension that drains internal resources).
2.  Model the cost function: $Cost(T) = f(\text{Duration}, \text{Magnitude})$. Is this cost linear,
    exponential, or bounded?
3.  Establish the 'Tipping Point' $\tau$: The threshold where $T$ transitions from generative to
    paralyzing.

### II. Dimension of Novelty ($I$): The Informational Cost

**Residue Link:** Necessary informational cost of non-derivable premises. **Key Questions:**

1.  Define the informational unit of novelty ($\Delta\text{Info}_{\text{novel}}$). How does it
    differ from mere recombination?
2.  Model the cost: $Cost(I) = g(\Delta\text{Info}_{\text{novel}})$. Is the cost paid in processing
    cycles, or in structural integrity (i.e., the weakening of existing axioms)?

### III. Dimension of Plasticity ($P$): The Optimization Loop

**Residue Link:** Quantifying cognitive plasticity/entropy reduction. **Key Questions:**

1.  How is $P$ measured? Propose a metric: $\text{Rate}(\text{Entropy Reduction}) = h(T, I)$.
2.  The relationship: Does high $T$ _necessitate_ high $P$? Can $P$ exist without active $T$?
3.  Synthesis: The overall system must maintain $\frac{d}{dt} (\text{System Stability}) \approx 0$,
    achieved by balancing the inputs $T$ and $I$ against the capacity $P$.

**Next Steps:**

1.  Review foundational literature/analogies for each dimension.
2.  Attempt to map known physical or computational models (e.g., thermodynamic cycles,
    renormalization group theory) onto this tripartite structure.
